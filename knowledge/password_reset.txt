Title: Password Reset and Account Unlock Guide
Category: Password Reset

[Section: Self-Service Reset Portal]
Description: Employees can reset their passwords autonomously using the Microsoft Self-Service Password Reset (SSPR) portal.
Steps:
1. Navigate to https://passwordreset.microsoftonline.com/ in a browser or mobile device.
2. Enter your full corporate email address and verify the CAPTCHA.
3. Complete the multi-factor authentication check using your registered phone number (SMS) or Authenticator App.
4. Set a new password. It must be at least 12 characters, including uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special symbols.

[Section: Helpdesk Assistance Reset]
Description: If you cannot access your SSPR methods, the IT Helpdesk can issue a temporary 24-hour temporary password.
Steps:
1. Contact the IT support hotline or request help from your manager to submit a ticket.
2. Verify your identity with the technician (employee ID number and manager verification).
3. The technician will reset your password in Active Directory and provide a temporary one-time password.
4. On your next login, you will be prompted to change this temporary password immediately.

[Section: Update Keyring and Keychain]
Description: After changing your password, your computer's local credential storage must be updated to avoid account lockout from old cached credentials.
Steps:
1. On macOS: Open Keychain Access, search for corporate account entries, and update the passwords or delete them so they prompt on next use.
2. On Windows: Open Credential Manager (Control Panel), click 'Windows Credentials', find corporate credentials, and select 'Edit' to update the password.
3. Restart your laptop and sign in with the new password while connected to the corporate network (or VPN) to update the cached profile.
