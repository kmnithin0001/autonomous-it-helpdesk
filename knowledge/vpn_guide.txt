Title: VPN Configuration and Troubleshooting Guide
Category: VPN Issue

[Section: Check Internet Connection]
Description: Before attempting to connect to the corporate VPN, verify that your local internet connection is active and stable.
Steps:
1. Open a web browser and navigate to a public site like google.com.
2. Run a speed test (fast.com) to ensure your latency is under 150ms and bandwidth is at least 5 Mbps.
3. If using Wi-Fi, try switching to a wired ethernet cable or move closer to the router.

[Section: Restart VPN Service]
Description: The local VPN service client (Pulse Connect or Cisco AnyConnect) may be hung. Restarting the client or the operating system service resolves 80% of issues.
Steps:
1. Close the VPN client application completely.
2. Open Windows Services (services.msc), locate 'Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility Client' or 'Pulse Secure Service', right-click and choose 'Restart'.
3. On macOS, open Terminal and execute: sudo launchctl kickstart -k system/com.cisco.anyconnect.vpnagentd
4. Re-open the VPN client and try connecting again.

[Section: Verify Credentials]
Description: Expired passwords or unsynced multi-factor authentication (MFA) tokens will cause the VPN connection to fail.
Steps:
1. Navigate to the corporate portal (portal.office.com) to verify that your active directory password is correct and has not expired.
2. Check your registered mobile device for any pending Duo or Microsoft Authenticator push notifications.
3. If your password was changed recently, clear your cached credentials in the VPN client settings and enter the new password.

[Section: Firewall Configuration]
Description: Local firewalls or public networks (hotels/cafes) may block the ports required for secure VPN tunnels.
Steps:
1. Ensure your local antivirus or Windows Defender Firewall is not blocking outgoing UDP ports 500 and 4500, or TCP port 443.
2. If connecting from a hotel or public hotspot, VPN protocols (IPsec/SSL) might be blocked by the network operator. Try connecting via a mobile hotspot.
