Title: Corporate Printer Setup and Spooler Troubleshooting
Category: Printer Issue

[Section: Add Network Printer]
Description: Connecting to the office shared network printer based on location.
Steps:
1. Windows: Go to Settings > Devices > Printers & Scanners. Click 'Add a printer or scanner'. Choose the printer named after your office room (e.g., 'PRN-NY-CONF-2B').
2. macOS: Go to System Preferences > Printers & Scanners. Click the '+' button. Select the IP tab, enter the printer hostname (e.g., ny-printer-2b.local), and select the protocol 'Line Printer Daemon (LPD)'.
3. Click 'Add' and confirm the default paper tray configurations.

[Section: Clear Print Queue]
Description: Stuck print documents halt all subsequent print jobs. Clearing the print queue resolves stalled printers.
Steps:
1. Open Printers & Scanners and double-click the active printer to see the queue.
2. Select any jobs showing 'Error' or 'Spooling' state, right-click, and select 'Cancel'.
3. If they won't clear, open Windows Services (services.msc), stop the 'Print Spooler' service.
4. Delete all files inside: C:\Windows\System32\spool\PRINTERS\
5. Start the 'Print Spooler' service again.

[Section: Driver Installation]
Description: Generic drivers might print gibberish characters or fail to utilize color and tray options.
Steps:
1. Identify the printer model (e.g., HP LaserJet Enterprise M608 or Xerox Altalink C8000).
2. Download the corporate-approved driver from the manufacturer portal.
3. Install the driver installer package. On macOS, choose the downloaded driver profile from the 'Use:' dropdown when adding the printer instead of generic 'AirPrint'.
